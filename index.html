If you have come to this page, it’s probably because experience a hard time getting up in the morning. Perhaps you also fall into the habit of snoozing and want to stop, because you know that every time you wake up and snooze, you restart your sleep cycle, making it increasingly harder to wake up.

This page is here to help you with this problem through guided steps. And if you never return to this page again, we hope it’s because we accomplished what we set out to do- helping you to get out of bed in the morning!

SUN

Our sleep and wake-cycle is regulated by the circadian rythm, an internal clock in our biology which is triggered by the appearence of light. The sunrise emulated on this page is thought to help you activate those cells and naturally wake you up.

WATER

There is a correlation between dehydration and energy levels. Since you loose fluids during sleep, a way to combat drowzyness is to hydrate first thing in the morning.

Pro tip: Set yourself up for sucess by having a glass of water by your bedside the day before.

OXYGEN

When you breathe deep, fresh oxygen will help regulate the circulatory system of your body. Fueling your brain with oxygen can help increase a sense of alertness.

STRETCH

Just like breathing, moving your body also helps your body’s circulatory system. Just a simple stretch first thing in the morning can aid you waking up.
